— Who we are

A quiet revolution, born in a small town in Andhra Pradesh.

Parivarthan — "transformation" — began with a simple conviction: that children who cannot hear should still be heard, and that families navigating disability should never walk alone.

The Integrated Rural People Welfare Society is a grassroots, multi-disciplinary non-profit serving children with hearing and speech impairments, families seeking guidance, and elders in need of dignified care. From classrooms to clinics to community homes, we build a barrier-free path forward — together.

— Stories of change

From silence to song. From isolation to belonging.

Chapter 01

The first word

Before

Arjun arrived at our school at age seven — bright-eyed but unable to express his name. His parents had been told he would never communicate.

After

Today, eighteen months later, Arjun signs full sentences, reads aloud in his own way, and teaches the younger children. His laughter fills the corridor.

Chapter 02

A voice, found

Before

Speech therapy can feel impossible — repetition, frustration, slow days. For many children, the first audible word arrives only after months of patient work.

After

Then it happens. A sound becomes a syllable. A syllable becomes a name. And a child finally hears themselves spoken into being.

Chapter 03

Evenings of dignity

Before

Many elders in our region face their later years alone — without family, support, or someone to share a meal with.

After

At our home, every evening is a small ceremony: warm food, shared stories, hands held. Care, the way it was always meant to be.
